# Why We Use Discord to Run a Plumbing Business (And Why It Works)

When most people hear "Discord," they think video games and internet communities. That's fair — that's how most people use it.

But over the last couple of years, a small number of trade business owners have started using Discord as the nerve center of their entire operation. Dispatching, crew communication, job alerts, customer texts, invoices, morning briefings — all running through one app they already know how to use.

Here's why it works so well, and why it might be the piece missing from your operation.

---

## The Problem with "Communication Chaos"

Ask most small contractors how their crew communication works and you'll hear something like: "I've got text threads everywhere, a Facebook group my guys barely check, Jobber for jobs, and I'm calling people half the day."

There's no central place. Things fall through the cracks. A job update lives in a text. A customer question is in an email. Your morning schedule is in your head.

When you're running two or three techs, this kind of friction costs real money — in missed follow-ups, wasted drive time, repeated phone calls.

---

## Discord Solves the "One Place" Problem

Discord is built around *channels* — separate spaces for different conversations. It's basically a walkie-talkie that also keeps records, sends files, runs bots, and integrates with everything.

For a trade business, we set it up like this:

**For the owner:**
- A morning digest at 7 a.m. showing today's jobs, flagged issues, and the weather — all in one message
- A channel where incoming customer texts appear and can be replied to without ever picking up your phone
- Alerts when a tech is late to a job, when a new quote is approved, when a payment comes in

**For the techs:**
- Their own private channel where they log arrivals, departures, job photos, and expenses
- Simple menu commands — tap a button to clock in, log a job, or flag an issue
- No complicated app to learn. If they know how to use their phone, they can use this.

---

## The Bot Does the Repetitive Stuff

The real magic is in the Discord bot — a piece of software we build that sits in your Discord and handles the routine tasks automatically.

When a customer texts in, it shows up in the right channel. When a tech takes a photo of a receipt, the bot reads it and logs the expense automatically. When a job is completed, the bot can trigger the invoice. When it's going to rain tomorrow, the bot flags it in your dispatch channel so you can plan around it.

None of this requires you to do anything except look at your phone when something needs attention.

---

## Why Not Just Use [Insert Other App]?

We get this question a lot. There are apps built specifically for field service businesses — why not just use one of those?

A few reasons:

**Cost.** Discord is free. The bot we build runs on your own hardware. The total ongoing software cost for a full setup is under $50/month.

**Flexibility.** Off-the-shelf apps do what they do and that's it. A custom bot does exactly what you need, the way your business actually works — not the way some software company assumes you work.

**Ownership.** When you use a third-party platform, your data lives on their servers. With this setup, your data lives on your hardware. You own it.

---

## Is This Hard to Learn?

Genuinely, no. We set it up, test it, and walk you through it. Your crew needs to know how to tap a button on their phone — that's about it. Most techs are using Discord within an hour.

The owner takes a little longer to get comfortable with all the channels and what they mean. But after a week, most people tell us it's faster than what they were doing before.
